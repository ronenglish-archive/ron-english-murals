London Pleasure Gardens, 2012

Group of large-scale installations created by Ron English for London Pleasure Gardens at the Royal Victoria Docks in 2012.

Description

Commissioned for the opening of the twenty-acre London Pleasure Gardens cultural site, Ron English installed multiple large-scale works across the grounds. His contributions included a repurposed jumbo-jet nose cone transformed into an oversized POPaganda creature and a long sequence of speech-bubble hoarding panels built around mythology, satire, and character-driven imagery.

These works appeared alongside installations by Shepard Fairey, TrustoCorp, RISK, and other artists, forming one of London’s major street-art activations during the 2012 Olympic year.
